# 1. Brainstorming & Ideation

## Problem Identified
Manual credit card approval decisions can be slow, inconsistent, and subject to human bias. Banks and financial institutions need a faster, data-driven way to assess whether an applicant is likely to be a low-risk or high-risk candidate for credit approval.

## Idea
Build a **Credit Card Approval Prediction** system — a machine learning-based web application that predicts whether a credit card application should be approved or rejected, based on the applicant's demographic and financial profile.

## Target Users
- Banks / financial institutions — automate initial screening of applications
- Loan/credit officers — get a fast, data-backed second opinion
- Applicants — understand what factors influence approval decisions

## Core Use Case
A user enters their **Gender, Annual Income, Income Type (employment sector), and Education Level**. The system predicts whether the application would be **Approved** or **Rejected**, along with a plain-language explanation of the factors that influenced the decision.

## Why Machine Learning?
Credit approval depends on multiple interacting factors (income, employment stability, education) that don't reduce to a simple rule. A trained classification model can learn these patterns from historical approval data more reliably than hand-written rules.
